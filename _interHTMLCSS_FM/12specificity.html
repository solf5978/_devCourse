I need more coffee

by Erika Lee

Ah, coffee. Whether you adore a whipped-topped Starbucks, a hot local cup of joe, or a cold brew as black as oil, or if you have children, like I do, you're probably addicted. I even own one of those mugs with the chemical formula for caffeine C8H10N4O2 emblazoned on the side.

You see, I was under the impression that I drank a lot of coffee, so I was surprised to read that, according to the International Coffee Organization[1], even Canada, in 10th place, is close to triple the coffee consumption of the average American.

The top spot for coffee consumption goes to Finland, followed closely by the heavily-caffinated Nordic countries of Norway, Iceland and Denmark.[2] Believe it or not, the average adult Finn drinks 27.5 pounds of coffee each year!
